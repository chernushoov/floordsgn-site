Substrate Preparation

Built for Abuse

PU-cement systems are thick-build mortars that require proper drainage design and substrate preparation.

  • Falls to drain — Minimum 1:80 fall required. We install screeds to create proper drainage
  • Shot blasting — Aggressive profile (CSP 5-7) for mechanical bond to substrate
  • Coved skirting — Integral cove at wall junctions eliminates corner joints
  • Channel drains — Stainless steel drainage integrated into floor system
  • Moisture tolerance — PU-cement is moisture-tolerant but excess water should be addressed

Technology

PU-Cement Science

01

Bonding System

Epoxy or polyurethane primer creates chemical bond to prepared concrete. Critical for adhesion under thermal stress.

02

PU-Cement Mortar

Polyurethane-bound cementitious mortar troweled to specified thickness. Contains special aggregates for thermal stability and wear resistance.

03

Seal Coat

Polyurethane topcoat seals surface, provides chemical resistance, and determines slip texture level.

Texture Options

Smooth (R10)

Easy to clean, moderate slip resistance. Dry processing areas.

Medium (R11)

Balanced cleanability and grip. Most food processing applications.

Coarse (R12)

Higher slip resistance for wet/oily areas. Slightly more effort to clean.

Heavy Duty (R13)

Maximum grip for extreme wet conditions. Industrial drainage areas.
