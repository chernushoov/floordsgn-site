Assessment Process

Evaluate Before We Restore

Not all floors can be restored. We conduct thorough assessment to determine if restoration is viable and cost-effective.

  • Adhesion testing — Pull-off tests verify existing coating adhesion. Delaminated coatings may need removal
  • Damage assessment — Extent of wear, chemical damage, mechanical damage documented
  • Moisture testing — Underlying moisture issues must be addressed before recoating
  • Compatibility — New coatings must be compatible with existing system. Unknown coatings may need removal
  • Cost-benefit analysis — We'll tell you honestly if replacement makes more sense than restoration

Process

Restoration Workflow

01

Assessment & Quote

Site visit, testing, documentation. We provide detailed report with options and honest recommendations. No-obligation quote.

02

Preparation

Diamond grinding or sanding to create profile for new coating. Repairs to cracks, chips, and damaged areas. Thorough cleaning.

03

Restoration

Application of primer (if needed), body coats, and topcoats. System matched to existing floor or upgraded as specified.

Advantages

Why Choose Restoration

Cost Effective

50-70% of replacement cost. Extends floor life at fraction of new installation price.

Less Downtime

Typically 1-3 days vs. 1-2 weeks for full replacement. Business continues faster.

Sustainable

Keeps existing floor out of landfill. Minimal new materials required.

No Demolition

No removal of existing floor. Less disruption, noise, and waste.

Upgrade Opportunity

Can upgrade to better topcoat, add slip resistance, or change color during restoration.

Preventive Maintenance

Regular recoating prevents wear-through to substrate. Extends total floor lifespan.

Maintenance Planning

Proactive Floor Care
