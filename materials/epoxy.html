Substrate Preparation

The Foundation of Performance

Epoxy systems bond mechanically and chemically to the substrate. Proper preparation is non-negotiable for long-term performance.

  • Shot blasting — Creates optimal surface profile (CSP 3-5) and removes laitance
  • Diamond grinding — For thinner systems or where shot blasting isn't possible
  • Crack repair — Structural cracks filled with flexible epoxy, hairline cracks with penetrating primer
  • Moisture testing — Calcium chloride and RH probe testing per ASTM F1869/F2170
  • Contamination removal — Oil, grease, curing compounds must be completely removed

Technology

System Architecture

01

Primer Coat

Low-viscosity epoxy penetrates substrate pores, consolidates weak surface layers, and provides chemical bond for subsequent coats. Coverage: 0.15-0.25 kg/m².

02

Body Coat / Broadcast Layer

Self-leveling or troweled epoxy mortar builds thickness. For high-traffic areas, quartz aggregate broadcast into wet resin creates mechanical interlock and wear resistance.

03

Seal Coat / Topcoat

Clear or pigmented topcoat encapsulates aggregate, provides UV stability (aliphatic systems), and determines final gloss level and chemical resistance.

System Variants

Self-Leveling

Smooth, seamless finish. 2-3mm thickness. Ideal for light-medium traffic, clean environments.

Quartz Broadcast

Colored quartz aggregate for texture and durability. 3-5mm. Heavy traffic, decorative options.

Mortar System

High-build troweled system. 6-12mm. Extreme impact and thermal shock resistance.

ESD/Conductive

Electrostatic discharge control. Electronics manufacturing, data centers, operating rooms.

Durability & Maintenance

Keeping Your Floor Performing

Epoxy floors are low-maintenance but not no-maintenance. A simple cleaning protocol extends service life significantly.

Daily

Dust mop or auto-scrubber to remove abrasive particles. Spot clean spills immediately.

Weekly

Wet mop with neutral pH cleaner (diluted). Avoid acidic or alkaline cleaners.

Quarterly

Deep clean with auto-scrubber. Inspect for damage, especially at joints and edges.

Annually

Professional inspection. Recoat high-wear areas as needed to maintain protection.
