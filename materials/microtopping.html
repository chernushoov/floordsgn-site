Substrate Preparation

The Canvas Matters

Microtopping is thin (2-3mm) so substrate imperfections telegraph through. Surface preparation determines final quality.

  • Existing tiles — Can be overlaid if sound. Grout lines filled, surface scuffed for adhesion
  • Concrete — Must be flat, free of laitance, cracks filled with flexible compound
  • Screeds — Self-leveling compound applied to achieve required flatness (SR1-SR2)
  • Bonding primer — System-specific primer ensures adhesion to various substrates
  • Crack bridging — Fiberglass mesh embedded at movement joints and over filled cracks

Technology

Application Process

01

Base Coat

First layer of polymer-modified cement applied by trowel. Builds foundation, fills minor imperfections. Fiberglass mesh embedded if required.

02

Finish Coat

Thin decorative layer applied and worked by hand to create desired texture — from smooth to varied, depending on technique and tools used.

03

Sealer System

Two-component polyurethane or epoxy sealer provides stain resistance and durability. Multiple sheen levels: matte, satin, or gloss.

Durability & Maintenance

Living with Microtopping

Properly sealed microtopping is durable and easy to maintain. The sealer does the heavy lifting — protecting the cement surface from stains and wear.

Daily

Sweep or vacuum to remove grit. Damp mop as needed with neutral pH cleaner.

Weekly

Wet mop with manufacturer-recommended cleaner. Avoid acidic or abrasive products.

Annually

Inspect sealer condition. High-traffic areas may need maintenance coat every 2-3 years.

As Needed

Spot repairs possible for localized damage. Full resealing recommended every 5-7 years.
