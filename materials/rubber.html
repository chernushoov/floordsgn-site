Substrate Preparation

A Solid Foundation

Rubber flooring can be glued, loose-laid, or installed over underlayment. Substrate requirements vary by method.

  • Full adhesive — Substrate must be flat (SR1-SR2), dry, and free of contaminants. Best for permanent installations
  • Loose lay — Heavier rubber tiles can be loose-laid. Allows for replacement and access to subfloor
  • Interlocking tiles — No adhesive required. Quick install for gyms and temporary applications
  • Poured rubber — EPDM granules bound with polyurethane. Seamless, any thickness, playground-rated
  • Moisture — Rubber is impermeable; excess moisture must be addressed to prevent mold beneath

Technology

Rubber Flooring Types

01

Vulcanized Rubber

Factory-produced sheets or tiles. Vulcanization process creates durable, resilient product. Available in natural or synthetic formulations.

02

Recycled Rubber

Made from recycled tires. Available as tiles, rolls, or poured-in-place. Excellent for gyms and playgrounds. Sustainable choice.

03

Poured EPDM

EPDM rubber granules mixed with polyurethane binder, poured on-site. Seamless, any thickness, any color. Ideal for playgrounds and sports surfacing.

Why Choose Rubber Flooring

Durability & Maintenance

Rubber Floor Care

Rubber flooring is low-maintenance and resilient. Proper care extends service life and maintains appearance.

Daily

Sweep or vacuum to remove grit. Damp mop high-traffic areas as needed.

Weekly

Machine scrub with neutral pH cleaner. Rinse well to prevent residue buildup.

Monthly

Deep clean and inspect for damage. Address cuts or tears promptly.

Annually

Professional cleaning if needed. Check seams and edges for lifting.
